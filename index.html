Types of Interview Questions

Prefer open-ended & probing (not leading, closed, or double-barreled)

Open-ended

Invites depth/story; “how/what/in what ways”.
Can you describe how access to transportation affects getting to clinic appointments?

Probing

Follow-up for more detail.
Can you say more about that? What happened next?

Demographic

Brief background context.
How would you describe your housing situation?

Closed

Yes/No or short choice.
Do you have health insurance?

Leading

Suggests a “right” answer.
Wouldn’t you agree that vaping is obviously harmful?

Double-barreled

Asks two things at once.
How do cost and transport affect appointments?

Hypothetical

Imagined scenario.
If the clinic moved farther, how would you handle visits?

Identify the Question Role

Pick one—definition and example will show below

Introductory

Warm-up to ease into the topic and establish context.
Can you tell me about alcohol use among your peers?

Core

Heart of the research; targets your main concept.
How do you think alcohol affects stress?

Follow-Up

Adds depth or clarification after a short/unclear answer.
Can you give an example?

Closing

Invites reflection, advice, or takeaway at the end.
What advice would you give a friend about alcohol and mental health?

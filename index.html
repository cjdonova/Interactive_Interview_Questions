Interview Question Builder

Use this step-by-step worksheet to draft a clear, neutral, open-ended interview question with an optional follow-up.

Step 0 — Your Research Topic / Question

What area or guiding question will your interview explore?

Step 1 — Choose a Focus (pick one)

Step 2 — Pick a Neutral, Open-Ended Stem

Choose one to drive your question:

  • How do you…
  • What happens when…
  • In what ways…
  • Can you walk me through…
  • Tell me about a time when…
  • What would you change about…
  • What matters most to you about…

Step 3 — Create an Example Question Using Your Stem

Rewrite the stem you chose in Step 2 into a full, single-focus, neutral question. Use blanks if needed.

Patterns you can copy:

  • How do you… → “How do you … when …?”
  • What happens when… → “What happens when …? Can you give an example?”
  • In what ways… → “In what ways does … affect …?”
  • Can you walk me through… → “Can you walk me through … from start to finish?”
  • Tell me about a time when… → “Tell me about a time when …. What happened?”
  • What would you change about… → “What would you change about …, and why?”
  • What matters most to you about… → “What matters most to you about …?”

Step 4 — Add a Possible Follow-Up (optional)

Use if answers are short. Examples:

  • Can you give a specific example?
  • What happened next?
  • How did that affect you?
  • Could you
